Ever Onward

ESTD. 1995

Bhoroli was the first girls' House to be established. It was founded way back in 1995 and continues to stand strong evermore. It has a long and passionate history, pregnant with good and bad memories all the same, beautifully shrouded with a spirit that surpasses the years it has stood by. The first House Captain was Saher Ahmed who led the House to victory then, as Suki leads us to victory now. Some things Bhoroli prefers to keep constant.

 

Our first Housemistress was Mrs. Sumitra Siromani, a fun-loving teacher of Biology with experience of teaching in several continents, ranging from Europe to Africa, Asia and back. She was accompanied by a stellar tutor group and an interesting bunch of students, including her own daughter, Shalini Siromani.

A fun fact of trivia, Roll No. 1 was a Bhorolite who went by the name of Supantha Gogoi. Also, Bhoroli was the only Girls' House at the time.

 

Bhoroli has travelled a long way, but one thing that remains is our love, spirit and devotion to the House. Bhoroli will continue to flourish, but it is our strong foundation and impressive history that shines the way towards an even brighter future.

 

 

Our Alumnis

 

TANYA ROSE RAO

Though a new face to AVS, she enlightened the house with her soccer and badminton skills. The house prefect of the house for 2015-2016, left a remarkable position for herself in the house. Never did we ever see her smile fade away from her face. Her stories and antiques made her unique. A great dramatist too. She was one of the best athletes Bhoroli had ever seen. She was an ideal among juniors.

 

SUKISUNGLA JAMIR

“Suki” as she was fondly called was the house goalkeeper in football and was one of the most lovable House Captains of all time. She was the one joking at all times and was loved by all. Her leadership left many stunned, she had tremendous respect from juniors and was everyone’s sister. This women was always part of the solution, never the problem.

 

KHRIEBUNUO ZUTSO

The kindest senior ever. She never raised her voice and was an inspiration for every junior. Soccer, Volleyball, Table Tennis, Basketball, Athletics, are just a few of the events and sports she represented the house in. She was the topper of the house in the ICSE and ISC examinations. She was awed for her all-round expertise. She contributed remarkably to the school and house.

 

YANGTILA OZUKUM

The Meghan Trainor of our house. Yangti was a renowned athlete and footballer whose performance in academics rose astonishingly. Her singing skills could be seen everywhere, be in the washroom or the big stage. To see her play, swim and run would be our favourite. She was indeed the fastest athlete among girls  in school. Her talent in art and design has deservedly placed her in among the best artists of the school. She was looked upon by juniors as a role model.

 

TANAVI CHOUDHURY

She was with us in Bhoroli only for 2 years but we felt as if we had known her since forever. When she laughed, everybody knew who it was! Her talent was seen when she played the Santoor. No one except her in school could play that instrument. She was a part of the waterpolo and swimming team of the house. She was an outstanding student. Her presence is missed in the house.

 

AYUSHI SAHARIA

No one could move the hip better than her. She was known for her cuteness, loving and carefree attitude towards the people around her. She was the biggest cricket fan that the house has ever seen. Her contribution in the field of academics has been vital. We miss her jokes even though if it could get lame at times.

 

ISHITA KASHYAP

Humour and Laughter is what defines her best. Never was she seen angry. Her words made great sense. She was not only kind but very generous too. She joined the school in class 11 but having her felt like she had been with us for years. She was seen as the most approachable senior by new comers and old comers alike. She was the Solomon of the house.

 

UNGERNA TOY

People knew her to be of a shy personality but that was only outside the house! She was a carefree student who played for the school soccer team. Una, as she was better known, was one of the best strikers that the house had ever produced. Her cool and carefree attitude was a part of her identity. She had been a mentor to us and a loving friend.

 

RABJA KEOT

She was someone whom we looked up to as an fashion icon. A very humble and kind natured woman. Bhoroli has never witnessed anyone who was sober like her. She was more than a senior to us. A great dancer and a fun loving girl who left extra ordinary memories in the house, will always be remembered by each girl of the house. She thought about the best ways for the upbringing of the girls to see the best in them.
